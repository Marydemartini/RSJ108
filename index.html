Mary DeMartini's Guide to Writing a Hard News Story

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make an awesome news story.

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

While “soft news” articles (such as interviews, human interest stories, and reviews) offer the writer more flexibility with structure and personal opinion, “hard news” articles follow a precise formula. Known as the “inverted pyramid,” this formula is designed to get right to the point. By sticking to the bare facts of your story, prioritizing those that are most important, and using your quotes effectively, you can inform your readers of what happened even if they don’t finish reading your article.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Pen
  • Paper
  • ipad or laptop
  • A relevant story
  • Facts and quotes
  • Patience

  1. Organize your Material.

    Using the inverted pyramid method, organize your facts from most the least important.

  2. Write a compelling lede.

    Remembering the inverted pyramid, write a 1-2 sentence paragraph to start off your story. It should be concise and include the most important information

  3. Add background information and quotes

    After the lede, contunue writing about the rest of the relevant information to the story, add quotes if possible.

  4. Tie it together with an interersting detail

    Finish the story with a fact or detail that is interesting, but not important to understanding the story

  5. Other Tips.

    Write in short paraghpahs, make sure to attribute sources correctly, and paraphase quotes when they do not add information or make a story more interesting.
